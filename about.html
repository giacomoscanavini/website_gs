Bio

Hi, I'm Giacomo!

I spent a long part of my life in particle physics, specifically studying neutrinos. During my master degree and later as a Ph.D. student I joined multiple collaborations based at the Fermi National laboratory (Fermilab) such as ArgoNeut, MicroBooNE, SBND, and DUNE. These collaborations leverage Liquid Argon Time Projection Chamber (LArTPCs) detectors to understand how these particles interact and oscillate.

As a postdoc I joined the Shah's lab to study brain computer interface (BCI) applications in humans, specifically in the context of motor-command following, and cognitive assessments methods for healthy and cognitive impared children and adults.

More recently, I have being contributing as an engineer to the development of a medical device with the purpose of measuring and tracking cognitive capabilities using auditory stimuli.
